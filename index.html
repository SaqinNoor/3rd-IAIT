THE BANGLADESH CONNECTION

How those cables connect to Bangladesh and what it means for people here

SOUTH-EAST ASIA / BANGLADESH

Three Cables, One Coastline

Bangladesh connects to the global internet through three submarine cable systems: SEA-ME-WE 4, SEA-ME-WE 5, and the upcoming SEA-ME-WE 6. They come ashore at Cox's Bazar and Kuakata.

INFRASTRUCTURE / HISTORY

From Isolated to Connected

Bangladesh got its first submarine cable in 2006 with SEA-ME-WE 4. Before that it relied on satellite links. International bandwidth has gone from 20 Gbps to more than 2,200 Gbps since then.

ECONOMIC IMPACT

Digital Bangladesh

Every Bkash payment, every video call, every export order processed online goes through these glass fibers on the ocean floor. They are not optional infrastructure.
